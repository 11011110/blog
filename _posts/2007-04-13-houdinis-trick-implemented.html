--- layout: post title: "Houdini's trick, implemented" date: 2007-04-13 17:52:00 tags: [tools, origami] --- Houdini's most famous magical act was as an escape artist, but one of his other tricks, which some coauthors and I wrote about some time ago, involved origami-like constructions, in which a piece of paper was carefully folded, then cut along a single line, and unfolded to produce complex shapes. Erik Demaine has evidence that this problem dates back at least to early-18th-century Japan. In our paper (and others roughly contemporaneously by different sets of authors) we showed that any polygon can be cut in this way.
