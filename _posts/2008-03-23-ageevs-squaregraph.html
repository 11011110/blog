Ageev, in his paper "a triangle-free circle graph with chromatic number 5" [Discrete Math. 152: 295â€“298, 1996], constructed a chord diagram with 220 chords that needs five colors in any coloring for which crossing chords are given different colors; this is the maximum possible number of colors needed for any triangle-free chord diagram. The dual graph of Ageev's arrangement is a large squaregraph.
