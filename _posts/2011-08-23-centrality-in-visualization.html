Why Maps Are So Annoying. Kevin Drum (usually a center-left US politics blogger) moves into information visualization, with the observation that people tend to think items are bigger/more important when they're grouped in the center of an image than when they're scattered around the periphery. This seems problematic for visualization techniques such as cartograms that are based around the idea that equal area means equal visual impact.
